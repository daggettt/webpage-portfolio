A little bit about me...

Like many folks during the summer of 2020, I found myself in need of a new hobby. With an unprecedented amount of free time - and existential dread - I decided to dabble with web design. This eventually led me down a Reddit rabbit hole wherein I was first introduced to The Odin Project (TOP).

TOP is a free full-stack web development course, and is constantly being updated by a supportive community of writers and coders - many of whom are former learners. I thought the program was incredible, and over the course of several months I completed TOP's Foundations Curriculum, which introduced me to:

  • HTML Best Practices
  • CSS Concepts like the Box Model, CSS Grid and Flexbox layouts
  • JavaScript Fundamentals
  • DOM Manipulation & Events
  • Command Line and Git Basics
  • How to write clean, concise, and accessible code

Beyond these skills, TOP taught me how to focus my creative energy into programming - something that I would have laughed at in February, 2020. And yet, despite the learning curve, I quickly realized how rewarding this pasttime could be. At this point, the pandemic had forced me into a part-time position and I could feel that my art career was beginning to stagnate. Although I was still enjoying my role as an arts-facilitator I quickly realized that the most exciting part of my day was coming home after a 5 hour shift and working through TOP's self-directed lessons and assigments.

I found that I liked the way programming forced me to break problems down into manageable blocks. I liked that every (frustrating) issue had a solution - as long as I knew what question to ask. And I loved how I could apply my problem-solving brain to any challenge thrown at me. I was starting to realize that programming problems required creative solutions.

Though I still work in arts & culture, I've begun to explore my interest in responsive design and accessible user-experiences. To hone these skills, I am planning to study:

  • UI/UX Design Principles
  • Web Accessibility Standards
